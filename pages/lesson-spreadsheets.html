Spreadsheets for Beginners — Excel & Google Sheets

Spreadsheets look intimidating but they follow simple logic. This lesson teaches you the basics of Excel and Google Sheets — from navigating cells to writing your first formula — in plain English.

What is a Spreadsheet?

A spreadsheet is a grid of rows and columns used to organise, calculate, and analyse data. It's the tool of choice for budgets, accounts, to-do lists, data tracking, and anything involving numbers or tables.

A spreadsheet is like a very powerful table. Each cell is a box where you can put text, numbers, or formulas. The magic happens when you write a formula — you tell the spreadsheet "add up all the numbers in this column" and it calculates automatically. Change any number and the total updates instantly. It's like having a calculator built into every cell.

Navigating a Spreadsheet

A spreadsheet is made up of cells arranged in rows (numbered 1, 2, 3...) and columns (lettered A, B, C...). Each cell has an address: column letter + row number. Cell A1 is the top-left cell. Cell B3 is column B, row 3.

Your First Formulas

Every formula in Excel/Sheets starts with an = sign. This tells the spreadsheet: "calculate this, don't just display it as text."

=SUM(A1:A10)
→ Adds all numbers from cell A1 to A10

=AVERAGE(B1:B5)
→ Calculates the average of B1 to B5

=A1+B1
→ Adds the values in cells A1 and B1

=A1*B1
→ Multiplies A1 by B1

=MAX(C1:C20)
→ Finds the highest number in that range

You track your monthly expenses in a spreadsheet: January to December in rows. In cell B14, you type =SUM(B2:B13) to calculate the total for the year. Now every time you update a monthly figure, the annual total updates automatically. Change January from 45000 to 52000 and the total adjusts in an instant.
