Computer Viruses & Malware — Complete Beginner's Guide

You've heard the word 'virus' — but what exactly is it? How does a computer get one? This lesson explains all types of malicious software and gives you a simple action plan to stay protected.

What is Malware?

Malware is a broad term for any malicious software designed to damage your computer, steal your data, spy on you, or extort money. A computer virus is one type of malware. Here are the main types:

TypeWhat it DoesExample
VirusSpreads by attaching itself to files; damages dataILOVEYOU virus (2000) — infected 50M computers
RansomwareLocks your files and demands payment to unlock themWannaCry (2017) — hit 200,000 computers globally
SpywareSilently monitors your activity and steals passwordsKeyloggers that record everything you type
AdwareFloods your screen with unwanted advertisementsPop-up ads that won't close
TrojanDisguises itself as useful software to gain accessFake antivirus programs that install real viruses
PhishingTricks you into giving up passwords (not strictly malware but equally dangerous)Fake bank login pages

How Do Computers Get Infected?

⚠️
Email attachments: Opening .exe or macro-enabled .docx files from unknown senders
⚠️
Fake downloads: Downloading software from unofficial sites (pirated apps, movies)
⚠️
Infected USB drives: Plugging in a found USB stick or one borrowed from an untrusted source
⚠️
Malicious websites: Clicking on fake "You've won a prize!" or "Your computer has a virus!" popups
⚠️
Outdated software: Unpatched security holes in Windows or your browser

In 2017, the WannaCry ransomware infected 200,000 computers across 150 countries in just one day. It exploited a known security hole in older Windows systems. Microsoft had released a patch fixing that exact hole two months earlier — but computers that hadn't been updated were vulnerable. A simple Windows update would have prevented every single infection.

How to Protect Your Computer (Free Methods)

1

Keep Windows Updated

Settings → Windows Update → Check for updates. Security updates patch known vulnerabilities that malware exploits. Do this at least monthly.

2

Use Windows Defender (Free & Built-In)

Windows 11 comes with Windows Defender — a capable free antivirus. Make sure it's turned on: Settings → Privacy & Security → Windows Security → check all areas show green.

3

Don't Click Suspicious Links

If an email or website is pushing you to act urgently ("Your account is suspended!", "You've won!"), treat it as suspicious. Go directly to the website instead of clicking links.

4

Only Download Software from Official Sources

Get apps from the Microsoft Store, the developer's official website, or reputable sources. Never download cracked/pirated software — it almost always contains malware.

Signs Your Computer May Be Infected

⚠️
Computer is suddenly much slower than usual
⚠️
Pop-up ads appearing constantly, even without a browser open
⚠️
New toolbars or programs you didn't install
⚠️
Browser redirecting to different websites than you intended
⚠️
Friends say they're receiving spam emails from your address

📌 Protection plan: Keep Windows updated · Windows Defender on · Never open suspicious attachments · Download only from trusted sources · Strong unique passwords
