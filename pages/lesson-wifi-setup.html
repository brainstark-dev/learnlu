How to Connect to WiFi — Step-by-Step Guide

Connecting to the internet is one of the first things you do with any new device. This lesson walks you through the process clearly and explains what to do when things go wrong.

What is WiFi?

WiFi is a wireless technology that lets your computer, phone, or tablet connect to the internet without using a physical cable. Your router (a small box provided by your internet company) broadcasts a WiFi signal in your home — your devices pick up that signal to get online.

WiFi is like a radio station and your device is the radio. The router broadcasts a signal (like a radio tower). Your device has a built-in WiFi antenna that receives that signal. Just like different radio stations have different names (99.5 FM, 102.3 FM), your WiFi has a name too — called an SSID (usually something like your internet provider's name or one you chose yourself).

Connecting to WiFi on Windows

1

Click the WiFi icon in the taskbar

Look at the bottom right corner of your screen. Click the WiFi icon (it looks like radiating signal waves 📶).

2

Choose your network

A list of available WiFi networks appears. Find your home network name (SSID) in the list and click it.

3

Enter the password

Type your WiFi password (also called WPA key or wireless key). It's often printed on the back or bottom of your router. Passwords are case-sensitive!

4

Click Connect

Your computer connects to the network. Next time it will connect automatically without asking for the password.

Common WiFi Problems and Fixes

ProblemMost Likely CauseFix
Can't find your WiFi networkRouter is off, or you're out of rangeMove closer to the router; check the router is powered on
Wrong password errorCaps Lock is on, or typoCheck the password on the router sticker; try typing it elsewhere first
Connected but no internetISP outage or router needs restartUnplug the router for 30 seconds, plug back in
Slow WiFi speedToo far from router, or congestionMove closer to router; use 5GHz band if available
Keeps disconnectingWeak signal or driver issueUpdate WiFi drivers; move router to a central location

The universal first fix: Restart your router. Unplug it from the power outlet, wait 30 seconds, plug it back in. Wait 2 minutes for it to fully restart. This fixes 80% of home WiFi problems.

WiFi Security — Important!

Never connect to unknown public WiFi (like "Free_Airport_WiFi" or "FreeHotspot") to do banking or shopping. Anyone can create a fake hotspot with an innocent-sounding name to steal your data. On public WiFi, stick to browsing only — no passwords, no banking, no shopping.
