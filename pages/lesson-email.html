Email for Beginners: How to Send, Receive & Organise

Email is one of the most important computer skills you can learn. It's free, instant, and used for jobs, banking, school, and staying in touch with family anywhere in the world.

What Is Email?

Email (short for electronic mail) is a way of sending messages from one person to another over the internet — instantly and for free. Unlike a physical letter that takes days to arrive, an email arrives in seconds, no matter where in the world the recipient is.

🧠 Think of it like this

Email is exactly like the postal service, but digital and instant. Your email address is your home address. Your inbox is your letterbox. Sending an email is like dropping a letter in the post — except it arrives in seconds, not days, and costs nothing.

Understanding an Email Address

Every email address has two parts, separated by the @ symbol (pronounced "at"):

yourname@gmail.com
↑ ↑
Your username Email provider

Common email providers include Gmail (Google), Yahoo Mail, Outlook (Microsoft), and iCloud (Apple). Gmail is the most popular and recommended for beginners — it's free and works on any device.

🌍 Real-World Examples

These are all valid email addresses:

  • adaeze.okonkwo@gmail.com
  • john.smith123@yahoo.com
  • myshop2025@outlook.com

No two people in the world can have the exact same email address — it's unique to you, like a phone number.

Parts of Your Email Inbox

When you open Gmail or any email app, you'll see these main sections:

📥

Inbox

Where new messages from other people arrive. Every email sent to you lands here first.

📤

Sent

A copy of every email YOU have sent. Useful if you need to check what you said.

📝

Drafts

Emails you started writing but haven't sent yet. Auto-saves so you don't lose work.

🗑️

Trash / Bin

Deleted emails stay here for 30 days before being permanently removed.

⚠️

Spam / Junk

Suspicious or unwanted emails are automatically sent here by the email provider.

⭐

Starred / Flagged

Important emails you've marked so you can find them quickly later.

How to Write and Send an Email

Every email has four main fields you need to fill in:

1

To: (Recipient's email address)

Type the email address of the person you are sending to. Make sure there are no spelling mistakes — one wrong character means it won't arrive!

2

Subject: (What the email is about)

A short title summarising your email. Example: "Job Application — Sales Assistant Position" or "Question about my order #12345". Always fill this in — emails without a subject look unprofessional.

3

Body: (Your actual message)

Write your message here. Start with a greeting ("Dear Mr. Johnson," or "Hello Ada,"), write your message, then close with a sign-off ("Kind regards," or "Thank you,") followed by your name.

4

Attachments (optional)

Click the paperclip icon to attach files — documents, photos, or PDFs. Most email providers have a 25MB limit per attachment.

🌍 Sample Professional Email
To: hr@company.com
Subject: Application for Customer Service Role
Dear Hiring Manager,

I am writing to apply for the Customer Service position advertised on your website. I have 2 years of experience in retail and am a confident computer user.

Please find my CV attached to this email.

Thank you for your time and consideration.

Kind regards,
Chidi Okafor
+234 801 234 5678

What Are CC and BCC?

When composing an email, you'll see two extra fields: CC and BCC. Here's what they mean:

FieldStands ForWhat It DoesWhen to Use
CCCarbon CopySends a copy to extra people. Everyone can see who else was CC'd.When someone else should be kept informed (e.g., your manager)
BCCBlind Carbon CopySends a copy but other recipients CANNOT see who was BCC'd.Sending to a group privately, or when privacy matters
🧠 CC vs BCC Analogy

CC is like sending a letter and writing all recipients' names on the envelope — everyone can see who else got a copy. BCC is like quietly slipping a copy under someone's door without telling the main recipient — they receive it but nobody else knows.

Email Safety: Spotting Fake Emails

Not every email in your inbox is genuine. Phishing emails are fake emails designed to trick you into giving away your password, bank details, or personal information.

⚠️ Warning Signs of a Fake Email
  • The sender's email address looks strange (e.g., support@paypa1.com instead of support@paypal.com)
  • It creates panic — "Your account will be deleted in 24 hours!"
  • It asks you to click a link and enter your password
  • Poor spelling and grammar throughout
  • It promises you money, a prize, or a job you never applied for
  • The greeting is generic — "Dear Customer" instead of your name
✅ Golden Rule

If an email asks you to click a link and log in, do not click the link. Instead, open a new browser tab and go directly to the website yourself (e.g., type www.paypal.com yourself). Legitimate companies never ask for passwords via email.

📌 Key Takeaways

  • Email is free, instant digital mail — your address is unique to you
  • Every email has: To, Subject, Body, and optional Attachments
  • CC = everyone sees the copy; BCC = private copy
  • Never click links in suspicious emails — go to websites directly
  • Check the sender's email address carefully before trusting any email
