Social Media Safety — What Every Beginner Must Know

Social media connects you with the world — but it also comes with real risks if you don't know the rules. This lesson teaches you how to enjoy social media while protecting your privacy, money, and safety.

What You Should Never Share Online

The most common social media mistakes beginners make — all of these put you at risk:

Never ShareWhy It's Dangerous
Your home addressBurglars use this to target homes (especially when you post holiday photos showing you're away)
Your phone number publiclyEnables scammers to target you with calls, SIM-swapping attacks
Photos of your ID, passport, or bank cardEnough information for identity theft
Your daily routine ("I'm at the gym every morning at 6am")Helps criminals know when your home is empty
Your children's school nameCreates risk for child safety
Login details or passwordsSelf-explanatory — never to anyone

Setting Up Privacy on Facebook

1

Who sees your posts?

Every post has an audience selector (🌐 Public, 👥 Friends, 🔒 Only me). Set most personal posts to "Friends" not "Public".

2

Profile privacy review

Settings → Privacy → Privacy checkup. Facebook walks you through who can see your profile, photos, and personal information.

3

Who can find you?

Settings → Privacy → How people find and contact you. Set "Who can send you friend requests" to "Friends of friends" to reduce spam.

A woman in the UK posted "Heading to Spain for 2 weeks!" publicly on Facebook. She tagged her home city. Burglars saw the post, knew her house was empty, and broke in. Her posts were set to "Public" — meaning anyone in the world could read them, including criminals who specifically search for holiday posts in target areas.

Common Social Media Scams

⚠️
"You've won a prize!" — You haven't. Real competitions don't contact you through DMs asking for bank details.
⚠️
Fake friendship/romance: Someone befriends you online, builds a relationship, then asks for money for an "emergency." This is extremely common.
⚠️
Investment scams: "Double your money in 48 hours!" — always fake. Real investments don't guarantee returns.
⚠️
Hacked friend accounts: A message from a "friend" asking you to click a link or send them airtime. Their account was hacked.

A Facebook friend you haven't spoken to in years sends you a message: "I'm stuck abroad and my wallet was stolen. Can you send me $200 via mobile money urgently?" What should you do?

Managing Screen Time Healthily

Social media is designed by the world's best engineers to keep you scrolling. It's not a character flaw to find it hard to stop — the apps are deliberately addictive. Set yourself a daily time limit using your phone's built-in screen time tools (Settings → Digital Wellbeing on Android, Settings → Screen Time on iPhone). Most people significantly underestimate how much time they spend on social media.

📌 Social media safety rules: Posts to "Friends" not "Public" · Never share location, address, or ID · Never send money to online contacts · Call friends directly to verify emergency requests · Enable 2FA on all social accounts
