How to Buy a Computer — A Complete Beginner's Guide

Buying a computer can feel overwhelming with all the technical jargon on the price tags. This lesson translates all those specs into plain English so you can make a confident decision.

First: What Will You Use It For?

The right computer depends entirely on what you plan to do with it. Answer these questions first:

Use CaseMinimum SpecsEstimated Budget
Basic: email, browsing, documentsDual-core CPU, 8GB RAM, 256GB SSD$200–$400
Everyday: video calls, YouTube, light workQuad-core CPU, 8–16GB RAM, 512GB SSD$400–$700
Work & creative: editing, design, gamingi5/Ryzen 5+, 16GB RAM, 512GB+ SSD$700–$1,200
Professional: video editing, 3D, programmingi7/Ryzen 7+, 32GB RAM, 1TB+ SSD$1,200–$2,500+

Key Specifications Explained

1

Processor (CPU)

Intel Core i3 (basic) → i5 (mainstream) → i7 (powerful) → i9 (professional). AMD Ryzen 3/5/7/9 follows similar tiers. For most people, i5 or Ryzen 5 is the sweet spot.

2

RAM

Minimum 8GB for everyday use. 16GB recommended if you run multiple apps or do video calls regularly. More RAM = smoother multitasking.

3

Storage

Always choose SSD over HDD — it makes the computer dramatically faster. 256GB for basic use; 512GB if you store lots of photos/videos. External drives can add storage cheaply later.

4

Display (for laptops)

1080p (Full HD) resolution as minimum. 13-14" for portability; 15-16" for comfort at a desk. IPS display gives better colours and viewing angles.

5

Battery Life (for laptops)

Look for 8+ hours claimed battery life. Real-world usage is typically 60-70% of the advertised figure. Check reviews for real-world battery tests.

Laptop or Desktop?

💻

Laptop

Portable — use anywhere · Built-in screen, keyboard, trackpad · Single purchase · Good for: students, people who work in different places · Battery lasts 4–12 hours · Harder to repair/upgrade

🖥️

Desktop

Not portable — stays on your desk · Better performance per dollar · Easier to upgrade (add more RAM, bigger drive) · Good for: home use, gaming, professional work · Always needs to be plugged in · Needs separate monitor, keyboard, mouse

Where to Buy and What to Watch Out For

✓
Authorised retailers: Always purchase from authorised dealers to ensure warranty coverage
✓
Warranty: Minimum 1 year manufacturer warranty. 2 years is better.
✓
Check reviews: Search the exact model on YouTube and tech review sites before buying
⚠️
Avoid: Deals that seem too good to be true. Refurbished is fine if from a reputable seller — but verify the warranty.

For basic tasks like email, browsing, and creating documents, what is the minimum RAM recommended?

📌 Best value rule: Get an SSD (not HDD), at least 8GB RAM, and an i5 or Ryzen 5 processor. This covers 90% of everyday needs comfortably.
