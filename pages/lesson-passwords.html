Strong Passwords — How to Protect Your Accounts

Your password is the lock on the door to your digital life. This lesson teaches you how to make passwords that hackers can't crack — and how to remember them without writing them on sticky notes.

Why Strong Passwords Matter

Hackers use automated tools that can test millions of passwords per second. A weak password like "password123" or "123456" (the world's two most common passwords) would be cracked in under a second. A strong password would take thousands of years to crack.

In 2023, a major data breach exposed 8 billion passwords online. Researchers found that "123456" appeared 3.5 million times. That means 3.5 million people had the same password — and all their accounts were instantly vulnerable to anyone with the leaked database.

What Makes a Password Strong?

✓
Length: At least 12 characters. Longer is always stronger.
✓
Mixed characters: Use uppercase (A-Z), lowercase (a-z), numbers (0-9), and symbols (!@#$%)
✓
No dictionary words: "sunshine" is weak. "5unsh!ne2024" is much stronger.
✓
Unique per account: Never use the same password on multiple sites
✓
No personal info: Avoid your name, birthday, or phone number

The passphrase trick: Pick 4 random words and join them. "PurpleTigerCooksBread" is 21 characters, easy to remember, and would take computers millions of years to crack. Add a number and symbol: "PurpleTigerCooksBread7!" is even better.

Password Managers — The Smart Solution

A password manager is an app that securely stores all your passwords in an encrypted vault. You only need to remember one master password. The manager fills in all your other passwords automatically.

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Two-factor authentication (2FA) adds a second layer of security beyond your password. After entering your password, you also need to enter a code sent to your phone (by text or app). Even if someone steals your password, they still can't log in without your phone.

2FA is like a bank vault that needs two keys. The bank manager has Key 1 (your password). You have Key 2 (your phone). The vault only opens when both keys are used together. One stolen key is useless without the other.

You enable 2FA on your Gmail account. A hacker gets your password. Can they access your Gmail?

📌 Password rules: 12+ characters · Mix letters, numbers, symbols · Unique per site · Use a password manager · Enable 2FA on your most important accounts (email, banking)
