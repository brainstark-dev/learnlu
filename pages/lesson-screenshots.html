How to Take Screenshots on Windows

A screenshot captures exactly what's on your screen as an image — perfect for sharing errors with tech support, saving information, or showing someone how to do something. Windows has several built-in screenshot tools.

Why Take Screenshots?

✓
Show a technical error to IT support — a screenshot is worth a thousand words
✓
Save an online receipt, ticket, or booking confirmation
✓
Capture content from a video that can't be downloaded
✓
Create tutorials or instructions by showing step-by-step screens
✓
Share what you're looking at in a message without typing a long description

Four Ways to Take Screenshots in Windows

1

Windows + Shift + S (Recommended — Snipping Tool)

The screen dims and a small toolbar appears at the top. Choose: Rectangular (drag a box), Freeform (draw any shape), Window (click a specific window), or Full screen. The screenshot is automatically copied to your clipboard — paste it immediately into an email (Ctrl+V), Word, or WhatsApp. The image is also saved to your Screenshots folder.

2

Print Screen (PrtSc) key

Captures the entire screen to the clipboard. Nothing appears to happen — but press Ctrl+V in any document or image editor to paste it. On keyboards with a Fn key, you may need to press Fn+PrtSc.

3

Windows + PrtSc

Captures the full screen AND automatically saves it as a PNG file in: This PC → Pictures → Screenshots. The screen flashes briefly to confirm. No clipboard step needed.

4

Alt + PrtSc

Captures only the currently active window (not the entire screen) to the clipboard. Useful when you only want to share one specific window without the rest of your screen showing.

Using the Snipping Tool (Built-in App)

Search "Snipping Tool" in the Start Menu to open the full app. It lets you take screenshots on a delay (useful for capturing dropdown menus that disappear when you click elsewhere), annotate screenshots with a pen, and save in different formats.

Scenario: Your banking app shows an error message. You want to send a screenshot to customer support. Press Windows + Shift + S → drag a rectangle around just the error message → release → open Gmail → compose email → Ctrl+V to paste the screenshot → send. Total time: 15 seconds.

Adding Annotations to Screenshots

After taking a screenshot with Windows+Shift+S, a notification appears in the bottom-right corner. Click it to open the Snipping Tool editor where you can:

✓
Draw — circle or underline important parts with a coloured pen
✓
Highlight — use the highlighter to mark important text in yellow
✓
Add text — type labels or explanations onto the image
✓
Crop — trim the screenshot to show only the relevant part

📌 Key Takeaways

✓
Windows + Shift + S = select any area of screen (best method)
✓
PrtSc = full screen to clipboard
✓

✓
