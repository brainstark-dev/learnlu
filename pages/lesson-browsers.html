Web Browsers — Chrome, Firefox, Edge & Safari Explained

Your browser is the window through which you see the entire internet. This lesson explains what browsers actually do, which one to use, and the features that will make your internet experience faster and safer.

What is a Web Browser?

A web browser is a program that lets you visit websites. When you type google.com or click a link, your browser fetches that page from a server somewhere in the world, reads the code, and turns it into the visual page you see on screen.

Think of the internet as a massive library with billions of books. Your browser is the librarian — you tell it what you want, it goes and finds it, and brings it back for you to read. Without a browser, you cannot access the library at all.

🔵

Google Chrome

The most popular browser in the world. Fast, works on all devices. Made by Google.

🦊

Mozilla Firefox

Free and open-source. Excellent privacy protection. Great alternative to Chrome.

🌐

Microsoft Edge

Built into Windows. Based on Chrome engine. Fast and saves battery on laptops.

🍎

Apple Safari

Built into all iPhones, iPads and Macs. Very fast and power efficient on Apple devices.

Key Browser Features

Every browser has the same essential features. Once you learn them in one browser, they work the same in all others.

FeatureWhat it doesShortcut
📑 TabsOpen multiple websites at once in one windowCtrl + T (new tab)
⭐ BookmarksSave websites to visit again easilyCtrl + D
🔒 Incognito ModeBrowse without saving history or cookiesCtrl + Shift + N
🔄 RefreshReload the current pageF5 or Ctrl + R
⬅️ Back / ForwardGo to the previous or next pageAlt + ← / Alt + →
🔍 Find on PageSearch for a word on the current pageCtrl + F

📌 Recommendation for most beginners: Use Google Chrome — it is the most widely supported, works on Windows, Mac, Android and iPhone, and most websites are tested on it first. If you care about privacy, use Firefox.

Key Takeaways

  • ✓A browser is the program you use to visit websites — Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari
  • ✓All browsers do the same job — fetch, read, and display websites
  • ✓Tabs let you have many websites open at once
  • ✓Bookmarks save sites you want to return to
  • ✓Incognito mode hides your browsing from your own browser history
  • ✓Chrome is the best choice for most beginners
