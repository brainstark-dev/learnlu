Managing Screen Time and Eye Strain — Healthy Computer Habits

Use computers for hours without hurting your eyes or back. Simple habits that make a big difference.

Understanding Eye Strain

Computer Vision Syndrome (CVS) affects 50–90% of heavy computer users. Symptoms: dry eyes, headaches, blurred vision, neck and shoulder pain. The main cause is not blinking enough — we blink 15–20 times per minute normally, but only 5–7 times when staring at a screen.

Blinking moisturises your eyes. When you stop blinking enough because you're focused on a screen, your eyes dry out and start to ache. Consciously reminding yourself to blink fully (not half-blinks) can relieve symptoms within minutes.

The 20-20-20 Rule

Every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away (about 6 metres) for 20 seconds. This relaxes the eye muscles that have been focused on the close screen.

Set a timer on your phone for 20 minutes. When it rings, look out a window at something distant. Do this consistently for one week and you will notice significantly less eye fatigue by end of day. There are also free apps like "Eye Care 20 20 20" that remind you automatically.

Ergonomics: Sitting Correctly

Poor posture while using a computer causes neck pain, back pain, and repetitive strain injuries that can become serious over time.

Monitor heightTop of screen at or slightly below eye levelLooking up strains neck; looking down too much causes back curve

Blue Light and Night Mode

Screens emit blue light which signals your brain that it's daytime, suppressing melatonin (the sleep hormone). Using screens for 1–2 hours before bed makes it harder to fall asleep.

✓
Windows Night Light: Settings → System → Display → Night Light. Enable and set to turn on automatically at sunset.
✓
Reduce screen brightness in the evening to 30–50% of maximum
✓
Stop using screens 30–60 minutes before bed for noticeably better sleep quality
✓
Dark mode: Switch apps to dark mode to reduce overall screen brightness
