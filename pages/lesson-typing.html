Learn to Type: Touch Typing Basics for Beginners

Most people type with two fingers and look at the keyboard constantly. Touch typing — using all 10 fingers without looking — is one of the most valuable skills you can develop. This lesson shows you how to start.

Why Learn Touch Typing?

The average person types at 40 words per minute (WPM) using the hunt-and-peck method. An average touch typist types at 60–80 WPM. A proficient touch typist can reach 100+ WPM. At work, this difference compounds into hours saved every week.

A person who types a 500-word email using hunt-and-peck at 25 WPM takes 20 minutes. A touch typist at 75 WPM takes under 7 minutes. If you write 5 emails per day, that's over 1 hour saved every single working day — purely from typing speed.

The Home Row — Your Starting Position

The home row is the middle row of the keyboard: A S D F G H J K L ;. Your fingers should rest here when not typing. Two physical bumps (on F and J) help you find this position without looking — feel for them now on your keyboard.

Left hand: A S D F (index finger on F) Right hand: J K L ; (index finger on J) Thumbs: Both thumbs rest on the SPACE BAR

The home row is like the starting position of a race runner in blocks. It's not where you stay permanently — you reach out to other keys and return. The F and J bumps are your starting blocks. Every finger has a "home" key it returns to after reaching for other keys.

Correct Typing Posture

✓
Sit up straight — spine against the chair back, not hunched forward
✓
Screen at eye level — your neck should be neutral, not tilted down or up
✓
Elbows at 90 degrees — forearms parallel to the floor, wrists slightly elevated
✓
Fingers curved — like holding a small ball, not flat on the keys
✓
Don't look at the keyboard — this is the most important rule. Even if it feels slow at first, force yourself.

How to Practice Effectively

The best way to learn touch typing is with free online tools. Here are the most effective:

✓
Typing.com — free structured course from beginner to advanced. Has lessons, tests, and games.
✓
Keybr.com — adapts to your weak letters and focuses practice there. Very effective.
✓
Monkeytype.com — clean interface, track your WPM and accuracy over time.
✓
10FastFingers.com — practice typing common words. Test your speed with a 1-minute timer.

The 20-minute rule: Practice touch typing for 20 minutes every day. Your speed will drop dramatically in the first week — this is normal. After 2–3 weeks of consistent practice, you'll be back to your old speed but with proper technique. After 1 month, you'll be significantly faster. After 3 months, touch typing will feel natural.

5 Quick Tips to Type Faster

1

Accuracy first, speed later

Focus on hitting the right keys with the right fingers. Speed comes naturally once accuracy is solid. Rushing leads to bad habits that are hard to fix later.

2

Keep your eyes on the screen

Resist the urge to glance at the keyboard. Cover your hands with a cloth if you have to. Your fingers need to memorise the positions, not your eyes.

3

Return to home row after every key

After pressing any key, your finger should snap back to its home row position. This is what enables fast, fluid typing.

4

Type every day, even just for 15 minutes

Consistency beats marathon sessions. 15 minutes daily for 3 months beats 3 hours on weekends.

5

Use both Shift keys

Use the left Shift for capital letters on the right side of the keyboard and vice versa. This keeps your hands balanced and your typing rhythm smooth.
