How to Buy a Computer — Beginner's Buying Guide 2025

What to look for when buying a laptop or desktop. CPU, RAM, storage, display, battery — explained in plain English with recommendations.

Laptop vs Desktop — Which Should You Buy?

  • Laptop: Portable, all-in-one (screen, keyboard, battery built in). Good for students, people who move around, and most everyday tasks. More expensive for the same performance as a desktop.
  • Desktop: Sits on a desk, needs separate monitor, keyboard, and mouse. More powerful for the same price, easier to upgrade and repair. Better for gaming, video editing, and office work in one location.
  • Recommendation for beginners: A laptop is usually the better starting choice — portable, self-contained, and suitable for learning all the skills in this course.

What Specs to Look For

  • Processor (CPU): The brain. For basic use: Intel Core i3 or AMD Ryzen 3. For comfortable everyday use: Intel Core i5 or AMD Ryzen 5. For demanding tasks: i7/Ryzen 7 or above.
  • RAM: 8 GB minimum for comfortable use in 2025. 16 GB is better and future-proof. 4 GB will feel slow with multiple apps open.
  • Storage: Get an SSD (not HDD). An SSD makes your computer start and open applications significantly faster. 256 GB SSD minimum; 512 GB is recommended.
  • Display: 1080p (Full HD) minimum. Larger is not always better — a 15.6" 1080p display is more comfortable for most people than a tiny 13" screen. IPS panels have better colour and viewing angles.
  • Battery life (laptops): Look for 8+ hours for genuine all-day use. Marketing claims are optimistic — real-world is usually 30% less.
  • Operating System: Windows 11 (most compatible), macOS (Apple only), ChromeOS (cheap, simple, cloud-focused). Windows is recommended for beginners in most countries.
🌍 Budget Recommendations (2025)
  • Basic budget (school/basic office): Intel i3/Ryzen 3 + 8GB RAM + 256GB SSD — around ₦200,000–₦350,000
  • Mid-range (comfortable everyday): Intel i5/Ryzen 5 + 16GB RAM + 512GB SSD — around ₦400,000–₦700,000
  • Performance (video editing, gaming): Intel i7/Ryzen 7 + 16GB RAM + 1TB SSD + dedicated GPU — ₦800,000+
✅ Most Important Tip

The single biggest upgrade you can make for performance is switching from an HDD to an SSD. If budget is tight, choose a lower CPU (i3) with an SSD over a higher CPU (i5) with an HDD — the SSD will make your everyday experience feel dramatically faster.

📌 Key Takeaways

  • Laptop = portable; Desktop = more power per naira. Laptop recommended for beginners.
  • Minimum specs in 2025: 8GB RAM + SSD (not HDD) + 1080p display
  • SSD is more important than CPU tier for everyday speed
  • Intel i5/Ryzen 5 + 16GB RAM + 512GB SSD is the sweet spot for most users
  • Windows 11 is the recommended OS for most beginners
