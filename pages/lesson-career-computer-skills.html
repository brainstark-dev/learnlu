Computer Skills for Your Career — What Employers Want in 2025

The essential computer skills that employers look for in every job, how to demonstrate them on your CV, and free resources to learn them.

Why Computer Skills Are Now Essential in Every Job

In 2025, computer literacy is no longer a specialised skill — it is a basic expectation in almost every professional role, from administrative assistants to doctors, teachers to business owners. Employers assume you can use email, create documents, and navigate the internet. The question is whether you have the additional skills that make you stand out.

🧠 Analogy

Computer skills are the modern equivalent of being able to read and write. A hundred years ago, literacy was a distinguishing professional skill. Today, everyone reads and writes — the question is how well. Similarly, basic computer use is now assumed. The question is whether you have the advanced skills (spreadsheets, data analysis, coding basics) that differentiate you from other candidates.

Tier 1: Skills Every Employee Must Have

  • Email — composing, replying, organising, attachments, professional tone
  • Word processing — creating and formatting documents (Google Docs or Microsoft Word)
  • Internet navigation — finding reliable information, evaluating sources
  • File management — saving, organising, naming, finding files; using cloud storage
  • Video calls — Zoom, Google Meet, Teams for remote meetings
  • Basic keyboard shortcuts — copy, paste, undo, save, bold, find

Tier 2: Skills That Make You More Hireable

  • Spreadsheets: Google Sheets or Excel — SUM, AVERAGE, sorting, basic charts, simple data tracking
  • Presentations: Google Slides or PowerPoint — creating clear, visual presentations
  • Typing speed: 40+ WPM is considered professional. Free practice at typing.com
  • Google Workspace: Docs, Sheets, Drive, Gmail, Meet — used in the majority of modern businesses
  • Social media management: For marketing, customer service, and community management roles

Tier 3: High-Value Specialist Skills

  • Data Analysis: Advanced Excel or Google Sheets — pivot tables, VLOOKUP, dashboards
  • Coding basics: HTML/CSS for web, Python for automation and data — opensdoors in every industry
  • Graphic Design: Canva (free) for basic design; Adobe Photoshop for professional work
  • Video Editing: CapCut (free), DaVinci Resolve (free), Adobe Premiere (paid)
  • Database basics: Understanding of how data is stored and retrieved
🌍 Free Resources to Build These Skills
  • Google Digital Skills for Africa (learndigital.withgoogle.com) — free, certificate included
  • typing.com — improve your typing speed for free
  • GCFGlobal.org — free computer, Word, Excel, and internet courses
  • Khan Academy — free computer science and programming basics
  • YouTube — free tutorials for every skill — search "Excel for beginners 2025"
  • Coursera / edX — free audit of university-level courses (pay only for certificate)
✅ How to List Computer Skills on Your CV

Be specific. Don't write "computer skills" — employers see that as filler. Instead write: "Proficient in Microsoft Excel (pivot tables, VLOOKUP), Google Workspace, and Zoom. Typing speed: 45 WPM." Specificity signals genuine competence.

📌 Key Takeaways — And Congratulations!

  • Basic computer skills (email, documents, internet) are expected in almost every job
  • Spreadsheets, presentations, and typing speed are high-value mid-tier skills
  • Coding, data analysis, and design skills open doors in tech-adjacent roles
  • Be specific on your CV — list exact programs and your typing speed
  • Free resources (Google Digital Skills, GCFGlobal, YouTube) can build all these skills at zero cost
