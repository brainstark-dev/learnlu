Computer Maintenance — Keep Your PC Fast and Healthy

Simple maintenance habits every beginner must do monthly to keep their computer fast, secure and running well for years.

Why Maintenance Matters

Like any machine, computers need regular care. Without it, they accumulate clutter, slow down, overheat, and become vulnerable to security threats. Five minutes of maintenance per week can extend your computer's useful life by years and prevent frustrating problems.

Monthly Maintenance Checklist

  • ✅ Install Windows Updates
    Go to Settings → Windows Update → Check for updates. Security updates close holes hackers exploit. Never skip these — they keep your computer safe.
  • ✅ Empty the Recycle Bin
    Deleted files sit in the Recycle Bin and still take up space. Right-click the Recycle Bin icon → Empty Recycle Bin.
  • ✅ Clear your Downloads folder
    The Downloads folder accumulates installers, PDFs, and files you no longer need. Delete anything you won't use again — it frees up significant space.
  • ✅ Run a virus scan
    Open Windows Security → Virus & threat protection → Quick scan. Confirms no malware has slipped through.
  • ✅ Restart the computer
    Many people never restart — they just put the computer to sleep. A full restart clears memory, applies updates, and refreshes the system. Do this at least weekly.
  • ✅ Back up important files
    Copy your most important files to an external hard drive or Google Drive. One backup is not enough — follow the 3-2-1 rule: 3 copies, on 2 different types of media, with 1 stored off-site (cloud).

Physical Cleaning

Dust inside a computer traps heat, and heat is one of the primary causes of component failure. Every 3–6 months:

  • Use a can of compressed air to blow dust out of keyboard keys, vents, and fans
  • Wipe the screen with a microfiber cloth (never paper towel or household cleaners)
  • Clean the keyboard with a slightly damp cloth — be gentle and never spray liquids directly on the device
  • Ensure your computer sits in a well-ventilated space — not in a closed cabinet
✅ Laptop Tip

Never use a laptop on a pillow, blanket, or carpet. These soft surfaces block the cooling vents on the bottom, causing the laptop to overheat. Always use on a hard, flat surface. A laptop cooling pad (costs a few hundred naira) extends laptop life significantly.

📌 Key Takeaways

  • Install Windows Updates monthly — they patch security holes
  • Empty Recycle Bin and clear Downloads folder to free up space
  • Restart weekly — don't just sleep/wake your computer indefinitely
  • Back up files to Google Drive or external drive regularly
  • Keep vents clear and never use a laptop on soft surfaces
