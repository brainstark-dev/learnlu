Video Calls for Beginners: Zoom, WhatsApp & Google Meet

Video calls let you see and talk to anyone in the world for free. Whether it's a family call, a job interview, or a work meeting — this lesson teaches you everything you need to make and join calls confidently.

What You Need for Video Calls

✓
Camera (webcam): Built into most laptops. For desktop PCs, buy an external USB webcam (very affordable — Logitech C270 is popular for beginners)
✓
Microphone: Built into most laptop cameras. External USB microphone or headset improves quality significantly
✓
Speakers or headphones: To hear the other person
✓
Internet connection: Stable broadband or 4G. Video calls use about 1GB of data per hour for standard quality

Joining a Zoom Meeting — Step by Step

1

Get the Meeting ID and link

The meeting organiser sends you a link (e.g. zoom.us/j/12345678) or a Meeting ID + passcode. Save these before the meeting time.

2

Click the link (or open Zoom and click Join)

If Zoom is installed, the link opens it automatically. First time? Go to zoom.us → Download → Zoom Client for Meetings. Install it. Free, takes 2 minutes.

3

Enter passcode if asked

Type the passcode provided. This prevents uninvited people from joining the meeting.

4

Choose audio and video

Zoom asks if you want to join with computer audio — click "Join with Computer Audio." Make sure your camera is on if it's a video call (click the camera icon at bottom-left).

Before any important video call: (1) Test your camera and microphone — Zoom has a built-in test under Settings → Video/Audio. (2) Check your background — a clean, plain wall is professional. (3) Make sure your face is well lit (light source in front of you, not behind). (4) Plug in your charger if on a laptop. (5) Close unnecessary tabs to free up CPU and RAM for smooth video.

Sharing Your Screen

Screen sharing lets other participants see exactly what's on your monitor. In Zoom: click "Share Screen" at the bottom → choose which window or your entire screen → click "Share." A green border appears around what you're sharing.

Before sharing your screen: close any personal windows, emails, or sensitive information. Everything visible on your screen will be visible to all participants. Also close browser tabs with private information.
