What is Antivirus Software and Do You Need It?

Understand what viruses, malware and spyware are, how they get on your computer, and how antivirus software protects you.

What is a Computer Virus?

A computer virus is a type of malicious software (malware) that attaches itself to legitimate files and spreads from computer to computer. Once active, it can delete files, steal passwords, slow your system, or lock your data for ransom.

🧠 Analogy

A computer virus works exactly like a biological virus. It sneaks into a host (your computer), hides inside normal-looking files (like a cold virus hiding in your cells), and then replicates and causes damage. Just like hand washing prevents biological viruses, good digital hygiene prevents computer viruses — and antivirus is your immune system.

Types of Malware

  • Virus: Attaches to files and spreads when you share them
  • Worm: Spreads automatically across networks without you doing anything
  • Trojan: Disguises itself as useful software (a game, a tool) but does damage when installed
  • Ransomware: Encrypts all your files and demands payment to restore them — very dangerous
  • Spyware: Secretly records everything you type (including passwords) and sends it to criminals
  • Adware: Floods your screen with unwanted advertisements, usually installed alongside free software

How Malware Gets on Your Computer

  • Downloading pirated software, movies, or games from unofficial sites
  • Clicking malicious links in emails or on social media
  • Plugging in an infected USB drive
  • Installing free software that comes bundled with hidden malware
  • Visiting unsafe websites that automatically download malware

What Antivirus Software Does

Antivirus software continuously scans your computer for known malware patterns, monitors suspicious behaviour, blocks dangerous websites, and quarantines (isolates) anything it finds. Think of it as a security guard that checks every visitor entering your building.

Do You Need to Pay for Antivirus?

For most people, the answer is no. Windows 11 and Windows 10 include Windows Defender — a built-in antivirus that is genuinely good and completely free. Keep it enabled and updated, and it provides solid protection for everyday use.

🌍 Free Antivirus Options
  • Windows Defender — built into Windows, always on, no setup needed
  • Avast Free — good free option with web protection
  • Malwarebytes Free — excellent for on-demand scanning

📌 Key Takeaways

  • Malware is any software designed to harm your computer or steal your data
  • Main types: virus, worm, trojan, ransomware, spyware, adware
  • Windows Defender (free and built-in) provides solid protection for most users
  • The best protection: don't download from unknown sources, don't click suspicious links
  • Back up your files regularly — ransomware is defeated if you have a backup
