USB and Bluetooth: How to Connect Devices to Your Computer

Connect any device to your computer — USB cables, Bluetooth headphones, wireless mice, and more.

What Is USB?

USB stands for Universal Serial Bus. It is the standard technology for connecting devices to your computer via a physical cable. USB ports are the rectangular or oval slots on the side of your laptop or desktop.

USB is the modern equivalent of electrical plugs and sockets — a universal standard that means virtually any device (phone, keyboard, mouse, external drive, camera) can plug into any computer. Before USB, every device had a different cable and connector, making everything confusing. USB simplified everything.

USB Types and Speeds

TypeConnector ShapeSpeedCommon Use
USB-AFlat rectangle (traditional)USB 3.0: 5 GbpsFlash drives, keyboards, mice, chargers
USB-CSmall oval (reversible)Up to 40 GbpsModern phones, MacBooks, fast charging
Micro-USBSmall trapezoidUSB 2.0: 480 MbpsOlder Android phones (being phased out)

USB-C cables can be inserted either way up — you never have to flip the cable. USB-A can only insert one way. If it doesn't go in, flip it 180°. (There's a running joke that you always try the wrong way first, then flip it, and try the wrong way again. It always works on the third try.)

What Is Bluetooth?

Bluetooth is a wireless short-range technology that connects devices without cables. It uses radio waves to create a direct link between two devices within about 10 metres. Common uses: wireless headphones, speakers, mice, keyboards, and transferring files between phones.

Bluetooth is like a private walkie-talkie channel just for your devices. Your phone and your wireless headphones both tune into the same private channel and communicate directly — no WiFi needed, no internet required, just two devices talking directly to each other.

Pairing Bluetooth Devices (Windows)

1

Put your device in pairing mode

On your headphones or speaker, hold the Bluetooth button until the light flashes — usually blue and red alternating. This means it's ready to connect.

2

Open Bluetooth settings on Windows

Settings (Windows+I) → Bluetooth & devices → turn Bluetooth ON → click "+ Add device."

3

Select your device from the list

Windows scans and lists nearby Bluetooth devices. Click your headphones/speaker name.

4

Click Connect

Windows pairs with the device. Next time, they connect automatically when both are on and in range.

📌 Key Takeaways

✓
USB-C is the modern standard — reversible and very fast
✓
Bluetooth pairs two devices wirelessly within ~10 metres
✓
To pair Bluetooth: device in pairing mode + Windows Settings → Add device
✓
Once paired, Bluetooth devices reconnect automatically
