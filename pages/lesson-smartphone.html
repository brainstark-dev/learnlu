Android vs iPhone — What's the Difference?

There are two main smartphone platforms: Android (made by Google, used by Samsung, Tecno, Infinix, Huawei, and many others) and iOS (Apple's system, used only on iPhones).

FeatureAndroidiPhone (iOS)
Price range$30–$1,500+ (wide range)$500–$1,500+

CustomisationHighly customisableMore controlled/consistent
File accessEasy USB file transferMore restricted

Installing Apps Safely

1

Android: Open Google Play Store

Search for the app name. Check: developer name (official apps list the actual company), number of downloads, and reviews. Tap "Install".

2

iPhone: Open App Store

Search for the app. Verify the developer. Tap "Get". May require Face ID or Touch ID to confirm.

Always install apps from the official store (Google Play or App Store) only. Third-party app stores and downloaded APK files (on Android) are the primary way malware gets onto phones. An app claiming to be "WhatsApp Plus" or "Free Instagram Followers" that's not in the official store is almost certainly malicious.

Managing Your Phone Storage

Phone storage fills up quickly — especially with photos and videos. Here's how to manage it:

✓
Enable Google Photos backup: Backs up photos to the cloud automatically, freeing phone storage
✓
Delete unused apps: Long press on app icon → Uninstall
✓
Clear app cache: Settings → Apps → select app → Storage → Clear Cache
✓
Check what's using the most space: Settings → Storage → see breakdown

Mobile Data vs WiFi — Managing Your Data Usage

Your phone can connect to the internet two ways: via WiFi (free, uses your home or office connection) or via mobile data (uses your phone plan's data allowance). Streaming video and downloading large files uses a lot of data.

Data-saving habits: Watch YouTube videos on WiFi, not mobile data. Download maps for offline use before travelling. Turn off automatic app updates on mobile data (Settings → Play Store → Auto-update apps → Over WiFi only). Background app refresh and auto-play videos are silent data killers.
