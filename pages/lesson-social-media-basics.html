Social Media for Beginners: Facebook, Instagram & WhatsApp

Social media connects billions of people worldwide. But for beginners, it can feel overwhelming. This lesson explains what each major platform does, how to set up an account safely, and the most important privacy habits.

What is Social Media?

Social media are websites and apps that let people create profiles, share content (text, photos, videos), and connect with others. Unlike traditional media where a few companies broadcast to everyone, social media lets everyone publish and interact with everyone else.

Social media is like a global community noticeboard. Anyone can pin their own message (post), react to others' messages (like/comment), or have conversations. Some noticeboards are for everyone (Facebook), some are photo-focused (Instagram), some are for short quick messages (Twitter/X), and some are for private messaging (WhatsApp).

Setting Up a Facebook Account Safely

1

Go to facebook.com → Create New Account

Enter your real name (you can search for friends easily), email or phone number, a strong password, birthday, and gender. Your birthday is used for age verification — it's not shown publicly by default.

2

Set Privacy Immediately

Before adding anything: Settings & Privacy → Privacy Shortcuts → set "Who can see your future posts?" to Friends Only. Set "Who can see your friends list?" to Friends or Only Me.

3

Add a profile photo

A clear, recent photo of your face. This helps friends recognise you. Optional — you can skip this if privacy is a concern.

4

Find and add friends

Use the Search bar to find people by name. Send a friend request. They must accept before they can see your posts and vice versa.

Critical Privacy Settings — Do These Today

The default privacy settings on most social media platforms are too open. Strangers, advertisers, and scammers can see more than you realise. Always review privacy settings immediately after creating an account.

✓
Never post your home address — not in your bio, not in check-ins, not in captions
✓
Never post when you'll be away from home — "on holiday for 2 weeks!" tells burglars your house is empty
✓
Never share financial information — no bank details, card numbers, or large purchase announcements
✓
Be careful with phone numbers — scammers collect phone numbers from public social media profiles
✓
Think before you post — screenshots last forever; employers check social media before hiring

Spotting Fake News on Social Media

Social media is flooded with misinformation — false stories shared as if they were real news. Here are quick checks:

✓
Does the headline seem shocking or designed to make you angry? That's often a clue it's misleading
✓
Check the source — is it a real news organisation with a verifiable website?
✓
Search the story on Google — if it's real, multiple credible outlets will report it
✓
Don't share before verifying — you could be spreading false information to your network

Your neighbour goes on holiday and posts "Away for 2 weeks! So excited!" on Facebook with their home address tagged. What is the risk?

📌 Key Takeaways

✓
Each social media platform has a different purpose — choose based on what you want to do
✓
Review privacy settings immediately after creating any account
✓
Never post your address, holiday plans, or financial information publicly
✓
Verify stories before sharing — social media spreads misinformation rapidly
