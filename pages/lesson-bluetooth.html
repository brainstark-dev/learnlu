What is Bluetooth? How to Connect Wireless Devices

Bluetooth lets your devices talk to each other wirelessly at short range — no cables, no WiFi needed. This lesson shows you how to connect headphones, speakers, keyboards, mice, and phones.

What is Bluetooth?

Bluetooth is a short-range wireless technology that lets devices communicate with each other directly — without needing a WiFi router or internet connection. It works over distances of up to about 10 metres.

Bluetooth is like a private walkie-talkie system between two devices. WiFi is like a radio station that broadcasts to everyone — your router sends the internet signal to all devices in your home. Bluetooth is different — it creates a direct, private channel between just two specific devices. Your headphones and your phone become a private pair, talking only to each other.

Common Bluetooth uses: wireless headphones/earbuds · Bluetooth speakers · Wireless keyboard and mouse · Connecting your phone to your car stereo · Smartwatches and fitness trackers · Wireless game controllers

How to Pair (Connect) Bluetooth Devices

Pairing is the process of connecting two Bluetooth devices for the first time. You only need to pair once — after that, they remember each other and connect automatically.

1

Put the device in pairing mode

For headphones: usually hold the power button for 5–7 seconds until a flashing light or voice says "Pairing mode" or "Discoverable". Check the manual if unsure.

2

Enable Bluetooth on your computer/phone

Windows: Settings → Bluetooth & devices → turn on Bluetooth. Phone: swipe down notification shade → tap Bluetooth icon.

3

Click "Add device"

On Windows: Settings → Bluetooth & devices → Add device → Bluetooth. Your computer scans for nearby devices.

4

Select your device from the list

Find your headphones/keyboard/speaker in the list. Click it. You may need to confirm a PIN (usually "0000" or "1234", or a PIN shown on both screens).

Common Bluetooth Problems

ProblemFix
Device not showing in scanMake sure device is in pairing mode (not just on); move closer; turn Bluetooth off and on again
Paired but won't connectRemove (unpair) the device and pair again from scratch
Audio crackling or cutting outMove closer to device; remove obstacles between devices; check for interference from WiFi router
"Can't connect" errorRestart both devices; update Bluetooth drivers in Device Manager
