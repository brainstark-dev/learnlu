Email for Beginners: How to Send, Receive & Organise

Email is one of the most important computer skills you can have. Job applications, bank statements, school communications, and business all rely on email. This lesson covers everything from your first message to professional organisation.

What is Email?

Email (electronic mail) lets you send messages, files, and images to anyone in the world instantly, for free. Each email address is unique — it's your personal postal address in the digital world. The @ symbol separates your username from the email provider.

Email is like the digital postal system. Your email address is your house address. The email server is the post office. Composing an email is writing a letter. Clicking Send is dropping it in the postbox. Your Inbox is your letterbox. The difference? Email arrives in seconds, not days — and it's completely free.

Setting Up a Gmail Account (Free)

1

Go to gmail.com

Open your browser and type gmail.com in the address bar. Click "Create account."

2

Enter your name and choose a username

Your username becomes yourname@gmail.com. Use your real name (or initials) for professional communication. Avoid funny names like "coolguy123" for work emails.

3

Create a strong password

At least 12 characters, mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Never use your name or birthday. Write it down in a safe physical location.

4

Add a phone number for recovery

This lets you regain access if you forget your password. Important — don't skip this step.

Parts of an Email

FieldPurposeExample
To:Recipient's email addressboss@company.com
CC:Carbon Copy — others receive a copy (all can see)colleague@company.com
BCC:Blind Carbon Copy — hidden copy (others can't see)yoursecondmail@gmail.com
Subject:Brief description of the email topic"Job Application — Marketing Manager"
Body:Your actual messageThe main text of your email
Attachment:Files attached to the emailCV.pdf, photo.jpg

When to use BCC: You're sending a newsletter to 50 people. If you put all 50 in the "To" field, everyone can see everyone else's email address — a privacy violation. Put all 50 in BCC instead. Each person only sees their own address in the To field.

Writing a Professional Email

1

Clear Subject Line

Make it specific and descriptive. "Job Application" is vague. "Job Application — Web Developer — Ada Okonkwo" is clear and professional.

2

Proper Greeting

"Dear Mr. Johnson," or "Good morning Sarah," — always start with a greeting. Avoid starting with "Hi" for formal emails to strangers.

3

Clear Body

State your purpose in the first sentence. Keep paragraphs short. Use plain, simple language — no need to sound overly formal or academic.

4

Professional Sign-off

"Kind regards," "Best wishes," or "Sincerely," followed by your full name. Add your phone number for business emails.

Sending Attachments

An attachment is a file you attach to your email — a CV, photo, document, or spreadsheet. The recipient can download and open it.

Gmail: Click the paperclip icon 📎 at the bottom of the compose window. Browse to your file and click it. Always check: (1) Is the right file attached? (2) Is the file size under 25MB (Gmail's limit)? For larger files, use Google Drive and share a link instead.

Organising Your Inbox

✓
Inbox Zero habit: Deal with each email when you open it — reply, delete, or archive. Don't leave thousands of unread emails building up.
✓
Labels/Folders: Create labels (Work, Personal, Finance, School) to categorise emails. In Gmail: Settings → Labels.
✓
Unsubscribe: Every marketing email has an "Unsubscribe" link at the bottom. Click it to stop future emails from that sender.
✓
Spam: Suspicious or unwanted emails land in Spam automatically. Check it occasionally — sometimes legitimate emails are wrongly filtered.
