Online Banking Safety — How to Bank Securely Online

Online banking is incredibly convenient — but financial scams are also the most costly type of cybercrime. This lesson gives you a clear framework for banking online safely.

Before You Bank Online — The Safety Checklist

✓
Use your bank's official app or type the URL directly — never click a link to your bank from an email or text message
✓
Check for HTTPS and the padlock 🔒 in the browser address bar before entering any login details
✓
Never bank on public WiFi (coffee shops, airports, malls) — use mobile data instead
✓
Log out completely when you're done — especially on shared computers
✓
Enable 2FA / OTP on your banking account — one-time passwords via SMS are an essential extra layer

Recognising Fake Banking Websites

You receive a text: "Your GTBank account has been suspended. Verify now: www.gtbank-secure-verify.com". This is a fake (phishing) site. Real banks use their main domain: gtbank.com, access.accessbankplc.com, zenithbank.com — never an unusual subdomain or different domain with the bank name buried in it.

Real Bank SiteFake/Phishing Site
www.zenithbank.comzenithbank.securelogin.com ❌
www.gtbank.comwww.gtbank-account-verify.net ❌
www.uba.comuba-online.com.ng.secure-verify.ru ❌

A simple rule: your bank's real website is one short domain (bankname.com or bankname.com.ng). If there are dashes, extra words, or unusual suffixes in the address, it is almost certainly fake. When in doubt, type your bank's name into Google and click the official result — or call your bank directly.

Common Banking Scams to Know

1

OTP Scam

Someone calls claiming to be from your bank, says there's suspicious activity, and asks for the OTP (one-time password) just sent to your phone. Never give your OTP to anyone — real bank employees never ask for it.

2

Fake Customer Service

Scammers call or message pretending to be bank agents. They ask for your BVN, PIN, or account password. No real bank employee will ever ask for your PIN or password.

3

Overpayment Scam

Someone sends you "too much money" by mistake and asks you to refund the excess. The original payment later bounces (was fraudulent). You have refunded your own real money to a fraudster.

Good Online Banking Habits

✓
Check your account statements weekly for any transactions you don't recognise
✓
Use a unique, strong password for your bank account — different from all your other passwords
✓
Enable SMS transaction alerts so you know immediately about every transaction
✓
Keep your bank's official contact number saved in your phone
✓
If you suspect fraud, call your bank immediately to freeze your account

📌 Banking safety mantra: Never give your PIN, OTP, or password to anyone · Never click email/text links to log in · Use mobile data not public WiFi · Log out when done
